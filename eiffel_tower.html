Eiffel Tower

Overview

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower located in Paris, France. It was constructed for the 1889 Worldâ€™s Fair and has since become the symbol of Paris and French culture.

Today, it is one of the most visited monuments in the world.

Historical Background

The tower was designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel and completed in 1889. Initially, many critics opposed its construction, considering it unattractive.

Over time, it gained acceptance and became one of the most admired structures in Europe.

Architectural Features

The tower stands approximately 330 meters tall. Its iron lattice structure was an engineering innovation of its time.

It features multiple observation decks offering panoramic views of Paris.
