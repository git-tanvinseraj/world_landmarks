Petronas Twin Towers

OVerview

The Petronas Twin Towers are iconic skyscrapers located in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. Upon completion in 1998, they became the tallest buildings in the world.

They symbolize Malaysia’s rapid economic development and modernization.

Historical Background

The towers were designed by Argentine architect César Pelli and completed in 1998.

They held the title of the world’s tallest buildings until 2004.

Architectural Features

The twin towers are connected by a skybridge on the 41st and 42nd floors.

Their design incorporates Islamic geometric patterns, reflecting Malaysia’s cultural heritage.
