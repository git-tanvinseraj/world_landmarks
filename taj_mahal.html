Taj Mahal

Overview

The Taj Mahal is a magnificent white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. It is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture.

It was built as a symbol of eternal love and remains one of Indiaâ€™s most famous landmarks.

Historical Background

The Taj Mahal was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Construction was completed in 1653.

Thousands of artisans and craftsmen worked on the structure, combining Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles.

Architectural Features

The monument is built entirely of white marble and features intricate carvings and inlaid gemstones. Its symmetrical layout and central dome create a striking visual harmony.

The surrounding gardens and reflecting pool enhance its elegance and grandeur.
