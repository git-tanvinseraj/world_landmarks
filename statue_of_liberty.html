Statue of Liberty

Overview

The statue of Liberty is one of the most recognizable monuments in the world. Located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, it has welcomed millions of immigrants arriving in the United States since the late 19th century. The statue symbolizes freedom, democracy, and hope.

It was a gift from France to the United States, representing the friendship between the two nations and their shared commitment to liberty.

Historical Background

The statue was designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi, with its internal iron framework engineered by Gustave Eiffel. It was officially dedicated on October 28, 1886.

At the time of its unveiling, the statue stood as a powerful symbol of political freedom and opportunity during a period of large-scale immigration to America.

Architectural Features

The statue is made of copper sheets supported by an iron framework. Including its pedestal, it stands approximately 93 meters tall.

Lady Liberty holds a torch in her right hand and a tablet in her left inscribed with the date of American independence.
